05 BALLOT MEASURES
MEASURE ER — LA COUNTY · Essential Services Restoration Act
County Measure
🚩
YOU PAY THIS TAX. A direct consumer sales tax increase of 0.5% on your purchases. Every $100 you spend at checkout costs you an extra $0.50. LA County currently has the highest combined sales tax of any major metro in the nation (already 9.5% after Measure A passed April 2026 — another half cent). This would push some cities in the county above 10.25%. Groceries, prescriptions, and medical equipment are exempt.
Shall LA County increase sales tax 0.5% for 5 years to fund healthcare and social services?
Raises ~$1B/year for 5 years (expires Oct 2031, cannot extend without re-vote). Direct response to Trump's "One Big Beautiful Bill" Medicaid cuts. 700,000+ county residents projected to lose Medi-Cal. 7 county public health clinics already closed in 2026 from $50M+ in lost funding. Includes independent oversight and public reporting requirement. Opposed by Howard Jarvis Taxpayers Association (which notes this is the second half-cent increase in under two years). Only supervisor to vote NO: Kathryn Barger. Current polls: 47% oppose, 45% support — too close to call.

TENSION: This directly conflicts with your high-taxes concern. LA taxes ARE too high, and the city/county DO manage money poorly. The question is whether you believe Trump's healthcare cuts have created a genuine emergency that requires this specific response — or whether the county should cut spending elsewhere instead of asking you for more at checkout.

RECOMMENDATION: YES — with eyes open. The 5-year sunset and independent oversight clause are meaningful guardrails. The alternative is clinic closures and ER overcrowding that affects you even with private insurance. But this is the one race where your fiscal instinct is 100% legitimate and a NO vote is a reasonable principled position.
MEASURE TC — CITY OF LA · Hotel Tax Implementation Expansion
City Measure
Shall the City expand the hotel/transient occupancy tax to cover short-term rental platforms?
Closes a tax loophole — applies the existing hotel tax rate to Airbnb/VRBO-type platforms that currently avoid it. No new tax rate is created. No increase for regular residents. Who pays: short-term rental hosts and their guests (largely tourists/business travelers). Levels the playing field between traditional hotels and platform rentals.

RECOMMENDATION: YES. This is tax equity, not a tax increase on you. Closes a loophole that lets platforms free-ride on city infrastructure while traditional hotels pay. No direct cost to you as a resident.
MEASURE TT — CITY OF LA · Hotel Tax Rate Increase
City Measure
Shall the City increase the hotel/transient occupancy tax rate?
Increases the tax rate paid by hotel guests (tourists and business travelers) when booking a hotel room in LA. You pay this only if you're staying in a hotel in LA — which as a resident you rarely do. Revenue to city general fund — relevant to LA28 Olympic visitor infrastructure.

RECOMMENDATION: YES. Visitor tax — burden falls on tourists and out-of-town business travelers, not residents. Revenue supports Olympic-era infrastructure (your priority #7). No direct cost increase to you as a 90034 resident.
